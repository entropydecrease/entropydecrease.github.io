The internet’s default answer to “why is the sky blue?” is “Rayleigh scattering”. And that’s not wrong, but it’s also not very useful. Simply knowing the name of something is very different from understanding it. But if names don’t constitute understanding… what does? My answer: having a model that allows you to make predictions. If you can reliably predict something, then you probably understand that thing. In this article, we’ll explore why the sky is blue – but we’ll go deep enough that, by the time you finish, you can predict what color the sky will be on other planets.

互联网上对于“天空为什么是蓝色的？”这个问题的标准答案是“瑞利散射”。这个答案本身没错，但却没什么实际用处。仅仅知道某个事物的名称，与理解它有着天壤之别。但如果名称并不能构成理解……那什么才可以？我的答案是：拥有一个能让你做出预测的模型。如果你能可靠地预测某件事，那你很可能就已经理解了它。在本文中，我们将探究天空呈蓝色的原因——但我们会深入探讨，等你读完，你就能预测其他行星上的天空会是什么颜色。

Let’s start by asking ourselves: what color SHOULD the sky be? 让我们先问自己一个问题：天空本该是什么颜色？

Or, one step further back, what color should anything be? 或者，再往前推一步，任何东西_应该_是什么颜色？

And the answer is: the color of anything is due to1 the wavelength of photons coming from that thing and hitting your eye. 而答案是：任何东西的颜色都源于1从该物体发出并射入你眼睛的光子波长。

When sunlight hits Earth’s atmosphere, most colors of photons pass through unencumbered. But blue photons have a tendency to ricochet around a lot. 当阳光照射到地球大气层时，大多数颜色的光子都能毫无阻碍地穿过。但 蓝色 光子往往会发生多次散射。

This causes them to disperse all throughout the atmosphere. They disperse so far and wide, and are so numerous, that you can look at any part of the sky on a clear afternoon and, at that moment, blue photons will be shooting from that point straight to your eyes.这使得它们分散到整个大气层中。它们的分布范围如此之广，数量又如此之多，以至于你在晴朗的午后看向_天空的任何一处_，在那一刻，都会有蓝色光子从那个方向直射入你的眼睛。

Therefore the sky is blue. 因此天空是蓝色的。

Most colors of light pass through the atmosphere relatively unencumbered. You only see them when you look at the sun, where they contribute to the whiteness of the sun’s light. Blue, however, bounces around a lot, getting spread all over the sky. Because blue photons hit our eyeballs from every angle of the sky, the whole sky appears blue.
大多数颜色的光都能相对不受阻碍地穿过大气层。只有在仰望太阳时才能看到它们，它们是太阳光呈现白色的原因之一。然而，蓝光会四处散射，遍布整个天空。由于蓝光光子从天空的各个角度射入我们的眼球，所以整个天空看起来是蓝色的。

What’s so special about blue? 蓝色有什么特别之处？

This is true and all, but it kicks the can down the road. Why blue? Why not red? 这话没错，但这只是把问题往后拖了。为什么是 蓝色 ？为什么不是红色？

In short, it’s because blue and violet have the closest frequencies to a “resonant frequency” of nitrogen and oxygen molecules’ electron clouds. 简而言之，这是因为蓝光和紫光的频率与氮、氧分子电子云的“共振频率”最为接近。

There’s a lot there, so we’ll unpack it below. But first, here’s an (interactive) demo. 这里面涉及的内容很多，我们会在下面详细拆解。不过首先，来看一个（交互式）演示。

This demo is a simplification. In reality, 99.999% of photons pass through (neither scattering nor absorbing), even at the resonant frequency. Pretty boring to watch! 这个演示是一个简化版本。实际上，即便在_共振频率_下，99.999%的光子也会直接穿过（既不发生散射也不被吸收），看起来会相当单调！

When a photon passes through/near a small molecule (like N2 or O2, which make up 99% of our atmosphere), it causes the electron cloud around the molecules to “jiggle”. This jiggling is at the same frequency as the photon itself – meaning violet photons cause faster jiggling than red photons. 当一个光子穿过或靠近小分子（如占地球大气99%的氮气或氧气）时，会使分子周围的电子云发生“抖动”。这种抖动的频率与光子本身的频率相同——这意味着紫光光子引发的抖动比红光光子更快。

In any case, for reasons due the internal structure of the molecule, there are certain resonant frequencies of each molecule’s electron cloud. As the electron clouds vibrate closer and closer to these resonant frequencies, the vibrations get larger and larger. 无论如何，由于分子内部结构的原因，每种分子的电子云都存在特定的_共振频率_。当电子云的振动频率越来越接近这些共振频率时，振动幅度会变得越来越大。

(This is completely analogous to pushing a child on a swing at the “right” frequency so that they swing higher and higher) （这与以“正确”的频率推秋千上的孩子，使其越荡越高的原理完全类似）

The stronger the electron cloud’s oscillations, the more likely a passing photon (a) is deflected in a new direction rather than (b) passes straight through. 电子云的振荡越强，经过的光子（a）就越有可能被偏转到新方向，而不是（b）直接穿过。

For both N2 and O2, the lowest resonant frequency is in the ultraviolet range. So as the visible colors increase in frequency towards ultraviolet, we see more and more deflection, or “scattering”2. 对于氮气（N₂）和氧气（O₂）而言，最低共振频率均处于紫外光波段。因此，当可见光的频率向紫外光方向升高时，我们会观察到其偏折程度（或称“散射”）愈发显著2。

Math talk: scattering increases proportional to the FOURTH power of the frequency. So higher frequency light means WAY more scattering. 数学原理：散射强度与频率的四次方成正比。因此，频率更高的光产生的散射要多得多。

So why isn’t the sky violet? Great question – we’ll cover that in a sec .那为什么天空不是紫色的呢？好问题——我们马上就来解释 。

I just want to point out two other things that (a) you can see in the demo above, and (b) are useful for later in this article.我只想指出另外两点：(a) 你可以在上面的演示中看到，(b) 这两点对本文后续内容很有帮助。

First, when light gets really close to – and eventually exactly at – the resonant frequency of the molecule’s electron cloud, it gets absorbed far more than scattered! The photon simply disappears into the electron cloud (and the electron cloud bumps up one energy level). This isn’t important for understanding the color of Earth’s sky… but there are other skies out there  😉首先，当光线_非常_接近——最终恰好处于——分子电子云的共振频率时，它被_吸收_的程度远大于_散射_！光子直接消失在电子云里（电子云也会上升一个能级）。这对于理解_地球_天空的颜色并不重要……但宇宙中还存在其他天空  😉

Second, did you notice that even red scatters some? Like, yes, blue scatters 10x more. But the sky is actually every color, just mostly blue/violet. This is why the sky is light blue. If white light is all visible colors of light mixed together equally, light blue is all visible colors mixed together – but biased towards blue.其次，你有没有注意到就连红光也会_散射_一些？没错，蓝光的散射量是红光的10倍_还要多_。但天空实际上呈现_各种_颜色，只是_主要_是蓝/紫色。这就是天空呈_浅_蓝色的原因。如果_白_光是所有可见光等量混合而成的，那么_浅蓝_光也是所有可见光混合而成的——只是偏向蓝色。

What would the sky look like if it was only blue? Check it out.如果天空_只_是蓝色的会是什么样子？来看看吧。

I’ll just end by saying, this dynamic (where scattering increases sharply with the frequency of light) applies to far more than just N2 and O2. In fact, any small gaseous molecule – carbon dioxide, hydrogen, helium, etc. – would preferentially scatter blue, yielding a blue sky at day.最后我想说的是，这种规律（即散射程度随光的频率升高而急剧增强）适用于_远不止_氮气和氧气这两种气体。事实上，_任何_气态小分子——二氧化碳、氢气、氦气等——都会优先散射蓝光，从而让天空在白天呈现出蓝色。

Why isn’t the sky violet? 为什么天空不是紫色的？

As you saw above, violet scatters more than blue. So why isn’t the sky purple? The dumb but true answer is: our eyes are just worse at seeing violet. It’s the very highest frequency of light we can see; it’s riiight on the edge of our perception.正如你刚才看到的，紫光的散射程度_比_蓝光更大。那天空为什么不是紫色的呢？一个简单却真实的答案是：我们的眼睛对紫色的感知能力要差得多。紫色光是我们能看到的频率最高的光，它正好处于我们视觉感知的边缘。

But! – if we could see violet as well as blue, the sky would appear violet.但！——如果我们既能清晰看见蓝色，也能清晰看见紫色，天空就会呈现出紫色。

We might as well tackle the elephant in the room: if we could see ultraviolet (which is the next higher frequency after violet), would the sky actually be ultraviolet?我们不妨直面这个显而易见的问题：如果我们能看见_紫外线_（这是紫光之后频率更高的光），天空真的会呈现出紫外线的颜色吗？

And the answer is not really. If we could see UV, the sky would be a UV-tinted violet, but it wouldn’t be overwhelmingly ultraviolet. First, because the sun emits less UV light than visible light. And second, some of that UV light is absorbed by the ozone layer, so it never ever reaches Earth’s surface.而答案是_并非如此_。如果我们能看见紫外线，天空会呈现出带紫外线色调的紫色，但它不会是压倒性的紫外线颜色。首先，因为太阳发出的紫外线比可见光少。其次，部分紫外线会被臭氧层吸收，因此永远无法到达地球表面。

You can see both of those effects in the solar radiation spectrum chart:你可以在太阳辐射光谱图中看到这两种效应：

The sun emits the most visible light, with UV frequencies falling off very steeply. Augmenting this effect is that the ozone layer in particular absorbs a lot of UV before it can reach Earth’s surface. 太阳发出的可见光最多，紫外线频率则衰减得非常快。加剧这一效应的是，臭氧层尤其会在紫外线到达地球表面之前吸收大量紫外线。

Why is the sunset red? 为什么日落是红色的？

So the obvious next question is _why is the sky red at dusk and dawn?那么下一个显而易见的问题是_为什么黄昏和黎明时天空是红色的？

It’s because the sunlight has to travel through way more atmosphere when you’re viewing it at a low angle, and this extended jaunt through the atmosphere gives ample opportunity for allll the blue to scatter away – and even a good deal of the green too!这是因为当你以低角度观察阳光时，阳光需要穿过_厚得多的大气层_，而这段漫长的大气层之旅，给了_所有_蓝光充分散射消散的机会——甚至大部分绿光也会被散射掉！

Simply put, the blue photons (and to a lesser degree, the green) have either (a) gone off into space or (b) hit the earth somewhere else before they reach your eyes.简单来说，蓝光光子（以及程度较轻的绿光光子）要么（a）消散在了太空中，要么（b）在到达你的眼睛之前，已经撞击到了地球上的其他地方。

When the sun is on the horizon (e.g. sunrise or sunset), the photons it emits travel through 40x as much atmosphere to reach your eyes as they would at midday. So blue’s 10x propensity to scatter means it’s simply gone by the time it would’ve reached your eyes. Even green is significantly dampened. Red light, which hardly scatters at all, just cruises on through.当太阳位于地平线时（如日出或日落），它发出的光子到达你眼睛所需穿过的大气层厚度是正午时的40倍。因此，蓝光10倍的散射概率意味着它在本该到达你眼睛的那一刻就已经_消失了_。即便绿光也会被大幅削弱。而几乎不发生散射的红光则能顺利穿透大气层。

Why are clouds white? 为什么云是白色的？

The answer to this question is the second of three “domains” you should understand in order to have a working model of atmosphere color. The physics are different from the small-molecule scattering above.这个问题的答案是为了建立大气颜色的有效模型，你需要理解的三个“领域”中的第二个。其物理原理与前文提到的小分子散射有所不同。

Clouds are made up of a huge number of tiny water droplets3. These droplets are so small (around .02 millimeters in diameter) that they remain floating in the air. But compared to small gas molecules like N2 and O2, these droplets are enormous. A single water droplet may be 100 trillion H2O molecules! 云由大量微小的水滴组成3。这些水滴非常小（直径约0.02毫米），能够悬浮在空气中。但与氮气、氧气等小型气体分子相比，这些水滴_巨大_。单个水滴中可能包含十万亿个水分子！

So, it’s not as simple as “the photons cause the hundreds of trillions of electrons to jiggle”. Instead, it’s more like the light has entered a very tiny prism or glass bead. 所以，这并不像“光子导致数万亿电子发生晃动”那么简单。相反，情况更像是光线射入了一个非常微小的棱镜或玻璃珠。

In a prism, white light can reflect around, bounce off exterior or interior surfaces, and even reflect differently depending on frequency – creating a rainbow effect. 在棱镜中，白光可以发生折射，在棱镜的外表面或内表面反射，甚至会根据频率的不同产生不同的反射效果——从而形成彩虹效应。

The droplet is just as complex. Some of the photons hitting the droplet bounce off the surface. Some enter it, bounce around inside once, twice, etc. – and leave again. Perhaps a few are absorbed. As with a prism, different wavelengths of light will reflect at different angles. The specifics aren’t important – you should just get the general gist. 这个液滴同样十分复杂。一些撞击液滴的光子会从其表面反射出去。还有一些光子会进入液滴，在内部反射一次、两次甚至更多次——然后再离开液滴。或许还有少数光子会被吸收。就像棱镜一样，不同波长的光会以不同的角度反射。具体细节并不重要——你只需掌握其核心大意即可。

So whatever white (or slightly yellowish) light that came from the direction of the sun is leaving in many random directions. Think of every color, shooting off in different directions! And then multiply that by a quadrillion droplets! In sum, you just see every frequency of photon coming from every part of the cloud. 因此，所有从太阳方向射来的白色（或微黄色）光线都会向_随机_的多个方向散射。想象一下，每一种颜色都向不同的方向飞散！然后再把这个景象乘以千万亿个水滴！总而言之，你看到的就是云层的每一个部分都在发出各种频率的光子。

And that means the cloud is white! 这就意味着云是白色的！

This idea that the tiny droplets that comprise clouds scales up. Anything larger that light can enter – drizzle, raindrops, hail – will also tend towards white. 构成云的微小水滴这一概念会被放大。任何比光的波长更大的物质——毛毛雨、雨滴、冰雹——也往往会呈现白色。

But that raises the question – what about things in between tiny molecules (N2, O2) and the relatively enormous prism-like droplets? How do those things act? 但这就引出了一个问题——在微小分子（N₂、O₂）和相对巨大的棱柱形液滴之间的物质是什么？这些物质会如何运动？

Well, the dust in the sky of Mars is a great example 😉 嗯，火星天空中的尘埃就是一个绝佳的例子😉

Why is the sky on Mars red? 为什么火星的天空是红色的？

The answer to this question is the third of three “domains” you should understand in order to have a working model of atmosphere color. The physics are different from both the small-molecule scattering and large-droplet prism-dynamics above. 这个问题的答案是理解大气颜色工作原理所需掌握的三个“维度”中的第三个。其物理原理与上述的小分子散射和大液滴棱镜效应均不相同。

The Martian sky is red because it’s full of tiny, iron-rich dust particles that absorb blue – leaving only red to scatter. 火星的天空呈红色，是因为其中布满了富含铁的微小尘埃颗粒，这些颗粒会吸收蓝光——只留下红光进行散射。

Yeah, yeah, I hear you. This answer is can-kicking! “Dust, schmust. Why does it absorb blue?”, you demand. 好的好的，我明白你的意思了。这个回答就是在拖延问题！“灰尘，算了。它为什么会吸收 蓝色 ？”你质问道。

OK, so the answer is actually fairly straightforward. And it generalizes. Here’s the rule: whenever you have solid particles in the atmosphere (very small ones, approximately the size of the wavelength of visible light), they generally tend to turn the air warm colors – red, orange, yellow. 好的，答案其实相当简单，而且这一结论具有普遍性。规则如下：当大气中存在固体颗粒（非常微小，大小约与可见光波长相当）时，它们通常会让空气呈现出_暖色_——红色、橙色、黄色。

If you live in an area with wildfires, you’ve probably seen this effect here on Earth! 如果你住在有野火的地区，你很可能在地球上见过这种现象！

An orangish-brown smoke-filled sky above Palo Alto. Palo Alto 上空那片被烟雾笼罩的橙棕色天空

To really understand the reason, let’s back up and talk about some chemistry.要真正理解其中的原因，我们先回过头来聊聊一些化学知识。

Compared to tiny gas molecules, solid particles tend to have a much wider range of light frequencies that they absorb.与微小的气体分子相比，固体颗粒通常能吸收的光频率范围要广泛得多。

For instance, we discussed how N2 and O2 have specific resonant frequencies at which they hungrily absorb UV photons. Move slightly away from those frequencies, and absorption drops off a cliff.例如，我们讨论过氮气（N₂）和氧气（O₂）如何在_特定_共振频率下强烈吸收紫外线光子。一旦偏离这些频率，吸收就会急剧下降。

But even for a tiny dust nanoparticle, there are many constituent molecules, each in slightly different configurations, each being jostled slightly differently by its neighbors. Consequently, the constituent molecules all have slightly different preferences of which frequency to absorb.但即便是一颗微小的尘埃纳米颗粒，也包含_大量_组成分子，每一个分子都处于略有不同的结构状态，彼此间的相互碰撞也存在细微差异。因此，这些组成分子对吸收何种频率的偏好都_略有不同_。

Because the “peak” absorption of the molecules is usually violet or ultraviolet (as it is with small gases), blues/violets will make it to the surface much less than oranges/reds.因为分子的“峰值”吸收通常出现在紫光或紫外光波段（就像小分子气体那样），所以蓝光/紫光到达地表的量远少于橙光/红光。

Approximate light absorption from Martian dust as a function of wavelength 火星尘埃对光的近似吸收随波长的变化情况

Of course, a reasonable question is _why are blue and violet absorbed so strongly by these dust particles?当然，一个合理的问题是_为什么这些尘埃粒子对蓝光和紫光的吸收作用如此强烈？

Well, those are the only photons with enough energy to bump the dust molecules’s electrons up to a new energy state.嗯，只有这些光子具有足够的能量，能将尘埃分子的电子激发到新的能级。

(Reminder: a photon’s energy is proportional to its frequency. Higher frequency – e.g. violet – means higher energy, and lower frequency – e.g. red – means lower energy)（提醒：光子的能量与其频率成正比。频率越高，例如紫光，能量越高；频率越低，例如红光，能量越低）

So, the exact specifics depend on the molecules in question, but generally, the level of energy needed to bump up the electron energy state in a dust or smog particle’s molecules corresponds to violet or UV photons.所以，具体细节取决于所涉及的分子，但_一般来说_，提升尘埃或烟雾颗粒中分子的电子能级所需的能量水平，与紫光或紫外光子相对应。

This is actually true of solids in general, not just atmospheric dust or aerosols. If you’ve ever heard that purple was “the color of kings” or that the purple dye of antiquity was worth its weight in gold, it’s true! To get something purple, you’d need to find a material whose electrons were excited by low-energy red photons, but had no use for higher-energy violet photons.实际上，这一点适用于所有固体，而不仅仅是大气中的尘埃或气溶胶。如果你曾听说过紫色是“帝王之色”，或者古代的紫色染料价值连城（与等重的黄金相当），那这是真的！要得到紫色的物质，你需要找到一种材料，其电子会被低能量的红色光子激发，但对更高能量的紫色光子却没有反应。

A Tyrian purple shroud of Charlemagne 查理曼大帝的提尔紫裹尸布

So this is why the Martian sky is red – and why reds and browns are more common in nature (for solid things, at least) than purple and blue. 这就是火星天空呈红色的原因——也是在自然界中（至少对于固态物体而言）红色和棕色比紫色和蓝色更常见的原因。

Why is the Martian sunset blue? 为什么火星的日落是蓝色的？

It’s less famous than the red daytime sky of Mars, but the Martian sunset is blue! 它不如火星白天的红色天空出名，但火星的日落却是蓝色的！

Martian sunset photo taken by the Spirit rover. (Photo by NASA/JPL/Texas A&M/Cornell) 勇气号火星探测器拍摄的火星日落照片 (照片由美国国家航空航天局/喷气推进实验室/得克萨斯农工大学/康奈尔大学提供)

In the last section, we talked about Martian dust absorbing violet/blue. But the dust also scatters light – which it can do totally unrelated to how it absorbs (remember, since photons can – and usually do – cruise straight through a molecule, scattering and absorbing can have their own interesting frequency-dependent characteristics. They don’t simply sum to 100%)在上一节中，我们讨论了火星尘埃对紫光/蓝光的吸收作用。但尘埃还会_散射_光线——其散射方式与吸收方式_完全无关_（要记住，因为光子可以——而且通常会——直接穿过分子，所以散射和吸收各自具有独特的与频率相关的特性，二者并非简单相加为100%）

Small atmospheric particles, like dust and smog, are equal-opportunity scatterers. The absolute probability they’ll scatter a photon does not change significantly with the photon’s wavelength. However, different-frequency photons can be more or less likely to scatter in different directions. 像尘埃和烟雾这样的大气小颗粒是一视同仁的散射体。它们散射光子的绝对概率不会随光子的波长发生显著变化。然而，不同频率的光子在不同的_方向_上被散射的可能性可能有大有小。

For our purposes, it suffices to know that Martian dust – like many atmospheric particles of similar size – generally scatters blue light closer to the direction it was already going. Red light has a higher probability of deflecting at a greater angle. 就我们的目的而言，只需了解火星尘埃与许多其他类似大小的大气颗粒一样，通常会将蓝光散射到更接近其原本传播方向的位置。红光则有更高的概率以更大的角度发生偏折。

Because red light deflects MORE and blue light LESS when scattering off dust particles, the area directly around the sun will be blue – even though more blue is absorbed en route. 因为红光在尘埃颗粒散射时偏折得更多，蓝光偏折得更少，所以太阳正周围的区域会呈现蓝色——尽管途中会有更多蓝光被吸收。

When molecules deflect photons only a tiny angle, it’s called “forward scattering”. Forward scattering is the most pronounced for larger particles, like dust or smog aerosols. It’s actually so strong on Mars that even at midday, red light doesn’t fill the sky evenly – the sky opposite the sun is noticeably darker! 当分子使光子发生极小角度的偏折时，这种现象被称为“前向散射”。前向散射在尘埃、烟雾气溶胶等较大颗粒上表现得最为显著。在火星上，这种散射效应其实极为强烈，即便在正午时分，红光也无法均匀布满整个天空——背向太阳的那片天空会明显更暗！

But blue’s tendency to forward-scatter more directly against Martian dust means the Martian sunset has a blue halo. 但蓝色光更易向前散射的特性会在火星尘埃上形成鲜明对比，这使得火星日落呈现出一圈蓝色光晕。

Building a model 构建模型

At the beginning of this article, I said being able to predict something is a good measure of how well you understand it. Let’s do that now. Let’s build a model for predicting the sky color on new planets/moons, or during different scenarios on our own planet. 在本文开头，我曾说过，能够预测某件事，是衡量你对其理解程度的一个好标准。现在我们就来做这件事。我们来构建一个模型，用于预测新行星/卫星上的天空颜色，或是我们所在星球上不同场景下的天空颜色。

(Not the most practical thing, but a good nerdsnipe 🙂) （这不是什么特别实用的事，但绝对能让技术宅们上头🙂）

Here are the three general rules of thumb we’ve already talked about. 以下是我们之前讨论过的三条通用经验法则。

Small gas molecules = blue/green atmosphere 小型气体分子 = 蓝/绿色大气

Atmospheric gases tend to be much, much smaller than the wavelengths of visible light. In these cases, they tend to preferentially scatter blue/violet/UV. This means that gaseous atmospheres are usually blue or blue-green. 大气中的气体通常比可见光的波长短得多。在这种情况下，它们会优先散射蓝光/紫光/紫外线。这意味着气态大气通常呈蓝色或蓝绿色。

This is pleasingly true for Earth, Uranus, and Neptune4. 这一点在地球、天王星和海王星上都得到了很好的印证4。

Dust or haze = red/orange/yellow atmospheres尘埃或雾霾 = 红/橙/黄色的大气

When visible light hits particles that are in the ballpark of its own wavelength, things get more complicated and can differ on a case-by-case basis.当可见光碰到波长与其相近的粒子时，情况会变得更为复杂，且具体表现会因情况而异。

These particles are typically either:这些颗粒通常是以下两类之一：

  • Dust: solid particles kicked up by mechanical means (wind, volcanos, meteorites)尘埃：通过机械方式（风、火山、陨石）扬起的固体颗粒
  • Haze: solid particles formed by chemical reactions in the atmosphere霾：由大气中化学反应形成的固体颗粒

Dust and haze generally makes atmospheres appear warmer colors – e.g. red, orange, yellow. 尘埃和雾霾通常会让大气呈现出偏暖的色调——例如红色、橙色、黄色。

All three significantly dusty/hazy atmospheres in our solar system hold to this rule! 我们太阳系中这三个尘埃或雾霾显著的大气都遵循这一规律！

  • Mars’s sky is red due to iron oxide-rich dust火星的天空因富含氧化铁的尘埃而呈现红色
  • Titan’s sky is orange due to a haze of tholins (organic molecules)土卫六的天空因索林斯（有机分子）形成的雾霾而呈橙色
  • Venus’s sky is yellow to a haze of sulfurous compounds金星的天空因含硫化合物形成的雾霾而呈现黄色

Clouds = white/gray 云 = 白色/灰色

When visible light hits clouds of droplets (or ice crystals) that are much bigger than light’s wavelength, the droplets act akin to a vast army of floating prisms, sending out all colors in all directions.当可见光照射到远大于光波长的液滴（或冰晶）云团时，这些液滴就像一支庞大的漂浮棱镜大军，将各种颜色的光向四面八方散射出去。

Consequently, clouds tend to appear white, gray, or desaturated hues. 因此，云通常呈现白色、灰色或低饱和度的色调

(Provided the cloud is hit by white light from the sun, that is. If a cloud is below a thick haze or doesn’t receive all wavelengths, neither can it reflect all wavelengths) （前提是云受到来自太阳的白光照射。如果云位于浓厚的霾层下方，或者没有接收到所有波长的光，那么它也无法_反射_所有波长的光）

Putting it all together 综合来看

The largest and most complex atmosphere in our solar system is Jupiter. But we know enough to start making some smart guesses about it! 木星拥有我们太阳系中体积最大、结构最复杂的大气层。不过我们已经掌握了足够信息，可以开始对它做出一些合理的推测了！

Scattering: the bigger picture 散射：宏观视角

Scientists have official names for the three types of scattering we’ve talked about. I’d be remiss not to at least mention them:科学家们给我们讨论过的三种散射类型都起了正式名称。我至少得_提及_它们，否则就不够严谨了：

  • Rayleigh scattering: when the wavelength of light is much larger than the particle (e.g. visible light and tiny gas molecules)瑞利散射：当光的波长比粒子_大_得多时（例如可见光与微小气体分子）
  • Mie scattering: when the wavelength of light is the same order of magnitude as the particle (e.g. visible light and dust/haze)米氏散射：当光的波长与粒子处于同一数量级时（例如可见光与灰尘/雾霾）
  • Geometric scattering: when the wavelength of light is much smaller than the particle (e.g. visible light and droplets or ice crystals)几何散射：当光的波长远_小于_粒子时（例如可见光与液滴或冰晶的情况）

And yes, somewhat strangely, it’s not the absolute particle size that determines how it scatters light. It’s the relative size of the particle to the wavelength of light.没错，有点奇怪的是，决定粒子如何散射光线的并非其_绝对_粒径，而是粒子相对于光波长的_相对_尺寸。

This table implies that if you take a particle and shine longer and longer wavelength light on it, it’ll go from one domain to the next. And that’s true!这个表格表明，如果你拿一个粒子，用波长越来越长的光照射它，它会从一个领域过渡到下一个领域。事实确实如此！

This has some exciting implications. Say you have a thick smoke, opaque to the naked eye. Why not just look at the infrared range instead? If you use a long enough wavelength, smoke particles will be in the Rayleigh domain – where, of course, shorter wavelengths scatter much more than longer. So if we use a suitably long wavelength of infrared… the opaque becomes transparent. 这有着一些令人振奋的启示。假设你有一团肉眼无法穿透的浓烟。那为何不直接观测红外波段呢？如果采用足够长的波长，烟雾颗粒会处于瑞利域——在该区域，较短的波长自然比较长的波长散射得更强烈。因此，只要选用合适的长波长红外光……原本不透明的物体就会变得透明。

That’s what firefighters do, anyhow! 反正消防员们就是这么做的！

反正消防员们就是这么做的！

Smoke particles are in the Mie domain for visual light. Thick smoke can absorb enough light to become opaque. But in the infrared range, it’s the Rayleigh domain. There, long wavelengths mean less scattering. Less scattering means more transparency. And thus, infrared can see through smoke. 可见光下，烟雾颗粒属于米氏散射域。浓烟雾会吸收足够的光线而变得不透明。但在红外波段，烟雾则处于瑞利散射域。该波段中，波长越长，散射效应越弱；散射越弱，烟雾的透明度就越高。因此，红外线可以穿透烟雾。

But at least you know why the sky’s blue. 但至少你知道天空为什么是蓝色的了。
